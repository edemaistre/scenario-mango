Motion

Real clips, not a storyboard.

Five 9:16 clips, one per casting, each generated from an already-approved try-on still via Seedance 2.0 Fast. The first frame is the still you inspected, so the motion is provably the same garment and face. Drape and hem movement are what a flat image cannot sell.

9:16, 720p, ~5s, the everyday PDP and social format, ~146 credits each. A cross-model morph (one garment across faces) is an optional capability, held behind a garment-shape and face-stability QA pass before it represents the brand. Rollout clip volumes are projected at the platform rate (~120 to 400 credits per clip).

Accuracy, owned

The one that holds, and the one that does not.

C10 silent substitution

The brown blazer and aubergine trousers held. The shirt silhouette was substituted and the stripe washed out, an attractive image of the wrong product. That is why a human-QA-before-publish gate is mandatory, not optional. Every finding here was verified against its source packshot at zoom by a named reviewer.

C9 colorway win

A mislabeled SKU reproduced faithfully; only the source's very wide flare rendered slightly straighter. A sourcing and metadata lesson, not a Scenario miss, and we refuse to mis-confess it as one.

The acceptance rubric we propose

Lighting · styling · drape · print-scale · color-accuracy · silhouette. Plus a target share of images publishable unedited, agreed before the pilot.

Casting and pose

Same model, same Mango look, three poses generated by re-pose. Pose variety per model is a pilot deliverable; this is what the capability already produces, shown not claimed.

Honest scope on diversity

The cast spans Spanish, East Asian, Black, Northern European and South Asian faces, a represented cast for every product with no casting or travel. We say face deliberately: all five models share one slim build here.

Body-size and fit inclusivity, the dimension that actually drives fashion returns, is the headline pilot deliverable. In Phase 1 we render one look across XS-to-XXL builds to convert it from a claim into evidence.

The numbers

Unit economics, with the sources on the page.

50 on-model images for a reconstructed ~325 credits (a few dollars), in minutes. ~3 credits per try-on. A new casting is ~12 credits plus ~3 per swap, so representation is a parameter, not a budget line. Compute is cents; the cost that scales is human QA and retouch, which the C10 case proves is non-zero. Even fully loaded, a Scenario image lands an order of magnitude below a studio image.

Illustrative ROI: shoots avoided per category per year from the cost and speed delta. The pilot measures your real conversion and return figures against your own baseline; we assert no percentage here.
